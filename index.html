Our Mission Statement

"Our mission is to empower rural-urban migrants by raising awareness of accessible learning hubs and skills-building opportunities in Rwanda. We also aim to raise funds to support those who need help paying for these courses, so they can earn money and support their lives in urban areas."

Our Problem Statement

"According to World Bank data, 38% of Rwanda's urban population lives in slums. A large portion of these residents are rural-urban migrants who move to cities seeking better opportunities. However, many arrive without the skills or resources needed to secure stable incomes, and the high cost of living in urban areas makes it even harder for them to survive."

Our Solution

"We are committed to empowering rural-urban migrants in Rwanda by creating accessible learning hubs and skill-building opportunities that enable them to secure stable livelihoods in urban areas. Our mission is to bridge the gap between migration and employment by providing targeted training programs that equip migrants with the practical skills needed in the job market. We also aim to raise funds to offer financial support to those who cannot afford the cost of these courses, ensuring that everyone, regardless of their financial situation, has the opportunity to thrive in urban environments. By connecting migrants with the resources they need to build their skills and confidence, we are helping them break the cycle of poverty and create better futures for themselves and their families."

Our E-Lab Journey

Week One

Human Centered Design - Through the experiential session called Women of Dadaab, we were introduced to what it means to include a human perspective in our solutions.

Week Two

Introduction to Global challenges and Global opportunities -We were introduced to the Seven(7) global challenges and the seven(7) Global opportunities available.

Week Three

Generating A Mission -During this session, we chose one Global challenge and one global opportunity to develop a mission statement

Week Four

Problem Identification and Validation- We identified our wicked problem and also the root cause of the problem.

Week Five

Situation Analysis to scope a problem and PESTLE Analysis within our think tanks apply it to our mission

Week Six

We got to know how to identify people impacted by our project with the stakeholders analysis and learned more about the differents types of empathy through empathy mapping

Week Seven

After having gotten a better understanding of stakeholders and how they are either directly or indirectly linked to our mission, we were able to interview our own stakeholders as a think tank.

Week Eight

We analyzed the quantitative and qualitative data we had collected from the interviews we conducted in Week 7. This made it easy for us to draw conclusions and make a pitch deck.

Week Nine

​This week, we learned how to design a visually appealing pitch deck and how to pitch our solution to investors.
